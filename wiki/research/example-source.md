# Example Source Summary

> 🌱 **Seed page** — demonstrates the source-summary format for `wiki/research/`. Delete once
> real sources arrive.

When you ingest a source into `raw/`, I write a page like this: a faithful summary, its
credibility rating, and the connections it creates.

> **Conflict of interest:** none. *(If a source is promotional/affiliate/sponsored, that
> disclosure goes here, first — before any claim.)*

## Summary
2–4 sentences capturing what the source actually says.

## Credibility
Rated `high` because it's a primary source from a named author with evidence and no agenda.
(See the CRAAP checklist in [`METHODOLOGY.md`](../../METHODOLOGY.md).)

## Key claims
- A claim, with citation back to the raw file `(see [[raw/the-source.md]])`. `[C:high]`
- A claim resting on this source alone, not yet corroborated. `[C:med]`
- An inference of mine, worth checking. `[C:low]`

## Connections
How this source relates to, supports, or contradicts existing pages:
- Strengthens the thesis in [[synthesis]].
- ⚠️ Contradiction: this source says X `[C:high]`, but [[other-source]] said Y `[C:med]` —
  logged in [`questions.md`](../../questions.md).

## Open questions
- Things worth investigating next or a follow-up source to find — also add to `questions.md`.
